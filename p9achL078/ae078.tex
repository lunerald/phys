			hung on a dynamometer. Now the cube is dipped into a volumetric flask with water.
			\begin{enumerate}[a)]
				\item By how many ml does the water in the flask rise?
				\item What force does the dynamometer show before immersion?
				\item What force does the dynamometer show after immersion?
				\item The experiment is repeated, this time the measuring cylinder is on a scale. It shows in front of the \\
				Immersion $\SI{500}{\gram}$. What does it show after immersion?
			\end{enumerate}

		{\bf \item You want to check non-destructively} whether your new trophy is real silver.\\
		You have at your disposal:
		
		\begin{itemize}
			\item A large measuring cylinder
			\item A scale
			\item A chunk of real silver
		\end{itemize}
		
		\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item What properties must the cup, the measuring cylinder, the scales and the piece of silver have (size, quality, ...) in order for a check to be possible?
			\item Create instructions on how to proceed with your measurement.
		\end{enumerate}
		
		{\bf \item A hot air balloon contains $\SI{5000}{\meter^3}$ hot air.} It is $1\over 4$ lighter than the outside air, which has a density of $\SI{1.3 }{\kilo\gram \over {\meter^3}}$ has.
		\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item What is the maximum weight that the envelope with basket and crew can have for the balloon to fly?
			\item What volume would a helium balloon (density of helium: $\varrho=\SI{178}{\gram\over{\meter^3}}$) have to have with the same payload?
		\end{enumerate}
		
		
		{\bf \item Sodium polytungstate is a heavy liquid } with a density of $\varrho=\SI{3,1}{\gram\over\centi{\meter^3}}$. A stone with a mass of $\SI{80}{\gram}$ floats on it. $1\over 10$ of the stone is above the liquid surface, $9\over 10$ below. What is the volume and density of the stone?
