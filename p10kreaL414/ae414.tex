	{\bf circular movement}
	\begin{enumerate}[1.]
		
		%Task 1
		{\bf \item A ball is attached to a string of length $l=\SI{40}{\centi \meter}$ and with a frequency
			$f=\SI{1.5}{\hertz}$ thrown around.} It describes a horizontal circular path with constant angular velocity $\omega$.
		
		\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item Calculate the angular and orbital speed of the ball.
			\item When the string breaks, the ball is thrown horizontally. At what distance and at what angle does it hit the ground if it was at a height of $h=\SI{1.7}{\meter}$ at the time of tearing?
		\end{enumerate}
		
		{\bf \item Lunerald races along a dirt road on his e-bike.} His speedometer shows $\SI{36}{\kilo\meter\over \hour}$. At what frequency do the wheels turn if the wheel diameter is 28 inches?
		[1 inch $\approx \SI{2.5}{\centi\meter}$].
		
		{\bf \item An audio CD contains a recordable circular ring} with an outer radius of $r_a=\SI{5.8}{\centi\meter}$ and an inner radius of $r_i=\SI{2.2}{ \centi\meter}$ It is played from the inside out, with the speed of the reading head above the disk always being constant. The speed f is initially $\SI{500}{1\over\minute}$.
		\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item What is the speed at the end when almost the entire CD has been played?
			\item Estimate how "long" (in meters) the track read from the entire CD is, given a playing time of 74 minutes.
			\item How wide is this track then?
		\end{enumerate}
		
		
		{\bf \item The tachometer of Lunerald's sports car} shows $\SI{4500}{1\over\minute}$ while driving on the highway. The gear ratio is 3 to 1. How fast does Lunerald drive if the tire diameter of his car is 60 cm?
		
		{\bf \item The minute hand of the Big Ben tower clock } is 3 m long. What distance does it cover in one minute / one second?
		
		{\bf \item At what orbital speed} does the mouth of the Amazon (equator) move around the center of the Earth? [$R_{Aeq}=\SI{6378}{\kilo\meter}$]
		
		{\bf \item In a hydrogen atom, the electron} orbits the proton with an orbital speed of\\ $v=\SI{2,2e6}{\meter\over \second}$. $\SI{6,6e15}{}$ orbits take place every second. What radius can one assume for this atom?

		{\bf \item The speed of light $c$ can be measured using the Foucault rotating mirror method:}
		
		\noindent
		\begin{minipage}{0.65\textwidth}
			A laser beam is directed at a rotating mirror that rotates rapidly at $\omega$. The beam is reflected back at another mirror at a distance $d$ and reaches the rotating mirror again after a travel time $\Delta t = {2d \over c}$. This has now rotated further by an angle $\alpha=\omega \cdot \Delta t$. This creates an angle $2\alpha$ between the incident and exiting light beams, which can be measured.
			\begin{enumerate}[a)]
				\item In an experiment, the observed angle is $2\alpha=\SI{0.0040}{\degree}$.
				By what angle in radians did the rotating mirror continue to rotate?
				\item What is the speed of light $c$ if $d=\SI{8.0}{\meter}$ and the rotating mirror has a frequency of $f=\SI{100}{\hertz} $ rotates?
			\end{enumerate}
		\end{minipage}
