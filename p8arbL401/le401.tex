	\noindent{\bf Task 3. a)}\\
	\begin{itemize}
		\item Since the conversion of supplied energy $W_{zu}$ into useful energy $W_{nutz}$ is practically never complete, $W_{nutz} < W_{zu}$ and thus the quotient $\eta = {W_ {util} \over W_{zu}} < 1$
		\item This refers to the efficiency of the internal combustion engine, which can turn 100 units of chemical energy in the fuel into 40 units of mechanical energy (positional energy, kinetic energy). The remaining 60 units are "lost" as thermal energy (heat of combustion, heat of friction). You can also heat the passenger compartment with this heat, so part of this waste heat counts as useful energy and thus increases the efficiency.
	\end{itemize}
	\vspace{0.5 cm}
	
	\noindent{\bf Task 3. b)}\\
	The useful energy $W_{nutz}$ is the positional energy here:
	$$W_{nutz} = m\cdot g \cdot h = \SI{90}{\kilo\gram} \cdot \SI{9,81}{\meter\per\second^2} \cdot \SI{ 25}{\meter} = \SI{22}{\kilo\joule}$$
	The efficiency of the crane is this value, divided by the energy used $W_{zu}$:
	$$ \eta = {W_{nutz} \over W_{zu}} = {\SI{22}{\kilo\joule} \over \SI{25}{\kilo\joule}}= 0.88 = 88 \%$$
	\vspace{0.5 cm}
	
	\noindent{\bf Task 3. c)}\\
	The saw does not change its height during this time, so the crane does not do any work.
	
	\vspace{0.5 cm}
	
	\noindent{\bf Task 4.}\\
	The formula for the lifting work follows:
	$$W=F_G\cdot h \quad \Rightarrow \quad h = {W \over F_G} = {\SI{12000}{\joule} \over \SI{1500}{\newton}} = \SI{8}{\meter}$$
	\vspace{0.5 cm}
	
	\noindent{\bf Task 5. a)}\\
	The energy is calculated using the power times the sunshine duration:
	$$W_{auf} = \SI{1000}{\watt} \cdot 12 \cdot 60 \cdot \SI{60}{\second} = \SI{4,32e7}{\joule}\approx \SI{ 4e7}{\joule}$$
	\vspace{0.5 cm}
	
	\noindent{\bf Task 5. b)}\\
	We multiply the result by the efficiency:
	$$W_{nutz}= \eta \cdot W_{auf} = 0.175 \cdot \SI{4.32e7}{\joule} = \SI{7.56e6}{\joule} \approx \SI{8e6}{ \joule}$$

	\noindent{\bf Exercise 6. b)}
	We set electrical power equal to the quotient of work and time:
	$$
	\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{2}
	\begin{array}{rcll}
		P_{el} &=& {W_{pot} \over t} &|\cdot t\\
		P_{el} \cdot t &=& m g h &| :(mg) \\
		h &=& {P_{el} \cdot t \over m g } &=
		{\SI{2000}{\watt} \cdot \SI{240}{\second} \over \SI{5000}{\kilogram} \cdot \SI{9,81}{\meter\per\second^2 }}\\
		h &=& \SI{9,8}{\meter}
	\end{array}
	$$
