		%Task 1
		{\bf \item task}
		\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item Describe an experiment through which we can show in class that solid bodies expand when heated.
			\item Use the particle model to describe how the internal energy of this body changes
			\item How much does the length of a 30 m long railway track change when it is exposed to a temperature fluctuation between $\SI{-25}{\celsius}$ and $\SI{+40}{\celsius}$. --- A 1 m long steel rod changes its length by 0.012 mm when heated by 1 K.
		\end{enumerate}
		
		\begin{minipage}{0.65\textwidth}
			%Exercise 2
			{\bf \item A trough filled with 7.0 liters of water stands on a 50 m high tower (see sketch).} In the trough there is an agitator that is driven by a slowly sinking mass G of 100 kg in gear is maintained. The water in the trough begins to move violently and then calms down completely again.
			
			\begin{enumerate}[a)]
				\item What energy conversions occur in this experiment? --- Short presentation in the correct order!
				\item What temperature difference does the amount of water experience as a result of this process if around 60\% of the available energy is released to the water? [$c_w=\SI{4,18}{\kilo\joule\over \kilogram\kelvin} $]
			\end{enumerate}

		{\bf \item task}
		\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item Mr. Hot prepares himself a bath with 150 liters of water at the same temperature
			$\vartheta_2= \SI{38}{\celsius}$. The water to be heated had $\vartheta_1= \SI{14}{\celsius}$.
			How much does this bathtub cost if $\SI{1000}{\kilo\joule}$ costs around 4 cents?
			\item How much energy would Mr. Hot save when showering if the temperature of the water remained the same and about 25 liters of water were used to shower?
		\end{enumerate}
		
		{\bf \item The Berger family wants to make coffee late at night.} The machine holds 1.2 liters of water.
		\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item How much energy do you need for $\SI{80}{\celsius}$ hot coffee water if the water initially has $\SI{15}{\celsius}$?
			\item How long does the machine take if it can deliver the power of 800 W?
		\end{enumerate}
