	{\bf Electricity Science}
	\begin{enumerate}[1.]
		%Exercise 2
		{\bf \item The absent-minded "physicist" Dr. No idea claims:}
		
		\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item "The south pole of a switched-on electromagnet can be recognized by the fact that the iron core at this end is colored red."
			\item "This is how you recognize the negative pole of a direct current source: In an unbranched circuit with two identical light bulbs, the one that is closer to the positive pole of the power source shines brighter."
		\end{enumerate}
		What is wrong with these claims? Describe the correct procedure.
		
		{\bf \item Two thin, cylindrical iron rods are placed one behind the other in the interior of a long coil along the coil axis.}
		\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item Explain and justify what happens to the iron rods when current flows through the coil.
			\item Now you place the rods next to each other in the coil and turn on the power. What can be observed now?
		\end{enumerate}
		
		{\bf \item Electrical circuits: In a circuit with a power source and a light bulb should
			$\dots$}
		\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item $\dots$ is the current $\dots$
			\item $\dots$ is the operating voltage $\dots$
		\end{enumerate}
		be measured. Draw a circuit diagram for both cases
		
		
		%Task 3
		{\bf \item task}
		\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item Draw a circuit with a battery and two light bulbs connected in series.
			\item The current intensity on both light bulbs should be measured. State why a measuring device is sufficient and draw a new circuit with the measuring device.
			\item Now the voltages on both light bulbs should be measured. Draw a circuit that makes this possible.
		\end{enumerate}
		
		%Task 4
		{\bf \item task}
		\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item A notebook with the output $\SI{24}{\watt}$ is connected to a battery labeled $\SI{12}{\volt} \quad \SI{3000}{\milli\ampere\hour}$. How long can the notebook be operated with this battery?
			\item How many meters in altitude could an e-bike powered by this battery travel up a mountain if it weighs $ \SI{105}{\kilogram}$ with the rider and it is assumed that this energy is completely converted into positional energy?
		\end{enumerate}
		
	\end{enumerate}
