	\noindent {\bf task 2. a)}\\
	We calculate the forces occurring step by step from left to right. The force on the right lever arm is:
	$$F_2=F_G \cdot {a_1 \over a_2}=\SI{1500}{\newton} \cdot {\SI{1.50}{\meter} \over \SI{2.50}{\meter} }=\SI{900}{\newton}$$
	The rope force $F_s$ is half of that, i.e. $$F_s=\SI{450}{\newton}$$
	\vspace{0.5 cm}
	
	\noindent {\bf task 2. b)}\\
	According to the lever law, the force on the crank is:
	$$F_k = F_s \cdot {a_s \over a_k}=\SI{450}{\newton} \cdot {\SI{0.10}{\meter} \over \SI{0.40}{\meter} }=\SI{112.5}{\newton}$$
	\vspace{0.5 cm}
	
	\noindent {\bf task 2. c)}\\
	Half of the force $F_2$ acts on the cable anchorage A. So we bet
	$$F_2=2\cdot \SI{600}{\newton}= \SI{1200}{\newton}$$
	Now we calculate the lever arm $a_1$:
	$$a_1 = a_2 \cdot {F_2 \over F_G}=\SI{2.50}{\meter}\cdot {\SI{1200}{\newton}\over \SI{1500}{\newton}}= \SI{2,0}{\meter}$$
	\vspace{0.5 cm}
	
	\noindent{\bf Task 3. a)}\\
	The force on the nail is:
	$$ F_2 = {{a_1 \over a_2} \cdot F_1} = {\SI{0.15}{\meter} \over \SI{0.035}{\meter}} \cdot \SI{180}{\newton } = \SI{771}{\newton}$$

	\noindent{\bf Task 3. b)}\\
	Torque left:
	$$ M_1 = F_1 \cdot a_1 = \SI{6.0}{\newton} \cdot \SI{0.030}{\meter}=\SI{0.18}{\newton\meter} $$
	Torque right:
	$$ M_2 = F_2 \cdot a_2 = \SI{3.0}{\newton} \cdot \SI{0.080}{\meter} = \SI{0.24}{\newton\meter} $$
	The remaining moment is the difference of that, so
	$$ M_v = (0.18-0.24)\SI{}{\newton\meter} = \SI{0.06}{\newton\meter}$$
	\begin{minipage}{0.5\textwidth}
		This can be canceled out by an additional force that satisfies the following equation:
		$$ F_3 \cdot a_3 = \SI{0.06}{\newton\meter}$$
		So e.g.
		$$F_3 =\SI{3}{\newton}; \quad a_3 =\SI{0.02}{\meter}$$
		For example, this force must act to the right of the bearing and be directed upwards, as the moment on the right is larger and must therefore be weakened.
