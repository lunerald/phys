	\noindent {\bf Task 5. a)}\\
	Gay-Lussac's law states that the volume of an ideal gas is proportional to its temperature:\\
	$V \sim T $ or more precisely
	$$ V = V_0\cdot (1+ {T \over \SI{273,15}{\kelvin}})\qquad (T ~in~\SI{}{\kelvin}) $$
	This only applies if the pressure remains constant.
	\vspace{0.5 cm}
	
	\noindent {\bf Task 5. b)}\\
	According to the Boyle-Mariotte law, the pressure of an ideal gas is inversely proportional to the volume:
	$$ p \sim {1 \over V} \quad \textnormal{or} \quad p \cdot V = const.$$
	This product is therefore also proportional to the temperature. We use $R$ as a constant:
	$$ p \cdot V = R \cdot T $$
	This is the general gas equation.
	\vspace{0.5 cm}
