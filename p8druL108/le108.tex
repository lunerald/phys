	\noindent{\bf Task 2.}\\
	The density $\varrho_g$ of the gas is its mass divided by the volume:
	$$\varrho_g={m \over V} = {\SI{2,2}{\kilogram}\over\SI{3,1}{\meter^3}}= \SI{0.71}{\kilogram \over {\meter^3}}$$
	The density found is lower than that of air, which is why a balloon filled with this gas experiences a buoyancy force so that it can fly if its shell is not too heavy.

	\noindent{\bf Task 4. c)}\\
	Force = pressure times area:
	$$ F = p\cdot A = \SI{8e6}{\newton\over{\meter^2}}\cdot {600 \over 10000}~\SI{}{\meter^2} = \SI{480000}{\newton}=\SI{480}{\kilo\newton}$$
	This corresponds to a weight force that a $\SI{48}{\tonne}$ liquid column has.
