	\noindent {\bf task 2.}\\
	The Earth's orbit is an ellipse. Kepler's second law states that the traveling beam from the sun to the earth covers areas of equal size in equal periods of time, which means that the orbital speed of the earth is faster at points near the sun than at points far from the sun. The closest point to the Sun, perihelion, is reached on January 6th, while the furthest point from the Sun, aphelion, is reached in July. The earth therefore moves a little slower through the summer half of the year than through the winter half of the year.

	$$ T = {s \over v }= {\SI{2.9e19}{\kilo\meter}\over \SI{190}{\kilo\meter\over \second} } = \SI{1.5e17 }{\second} = \SI{4,7e9}{\annum} $$
