	\begin{enumerate}[1.]
		\item {\bf weight and mass}
		\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item Explain why a distinction is made in physics between weight and mass. (definition, connection, difference)
			\item You land your spaceship on an unknown exoplanet and are supposed to determine its location factor. The aids are a body with mass $m=\SI{150}{\gram}$ and an elastic spring with spring constant $ D=\SI{24.0}{\newton\over\meter}$. By adding the mass, the spring stretches by 13.0 cm.\\
			Calculate the force acting on the spring and the position factor of the planet.
		\end{enumerate}
		
		%Exercise 2
		\item {\bf Hooke's law, density}
		
		An aluminum cube with density $ \varrho_{Al}=\SI{2.7}{\gram\over\centi\meter^3}$ has an edge length of
		$b=\SI{4,5}{\centi\meter}$ . It is attached to a spring with spring hardness $D=\SI{0.9}{\newton\over\centi\meter}$, which it has a total length of $l=\SI{36}{\centi\meter }$ stretches. \\
		Calculate the mass of the cube and the length of the spring in the unloaded state.
		
		
		\item {\bf Hooke's law}
		\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item You should investigate whether a coil spring obeys Hooke's law up to the maximum load of $F_{max}=\SI{12}{\newton}$. How do you proceed?
			\item One spring has the spring hardness $ D_1=\SI{2.5}{\newton\over\centi\meter}$, a second has the hardness $ D_2=\SI{5.0}{\newton\over \centi\meter}$.
			\begin{itemize}
				\item What total elongation results if you hang the two springs one behind the other and load them with $F=\SI{10}{\newton}$?
				\item What spring hardness $D_3$ does this combination have?
			\end{itemize}
		\end{enumerate}

How often does he have to drive to the hardware store if his trailer has a payload of $\SI{0.6}{\tonne}$? $[ \varrho_{Concrete}=\SI{2,4}{\gram\over \centi{\meter^3}} ]$
		
	\end{enumerate}
