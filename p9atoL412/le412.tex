		\item
		The law of decay applies:
		$$ A(t)=A_0 \cdot \left( {1 \over 2} \right)^{t \over T_{1/2}}$$
		Here $A(t)$ is the activity after the elapsed time $t$,
		$A_0$ is the initial activity at time $t=0$ and $T_{1/2}$ is the half-life. The elapsed time and the half-life must be used in the same unit. This results in:
		$$ A(t)=\SI{2.5e3}{\becquerel}\cdot \left( {1 \over 2} \right)^{2020-1966 \over 29~\rm{a}}= \SI {688}{\becquerel}$$
		
		\item
		75\% has decayed when $1\over 4$ of the original amount is still present. So $${1\over 4} = \left({1\over 2}\right)^2$$ two half-lives have passed.
