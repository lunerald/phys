	{\bf Wave exercise sheet}\\
	\begin{enumerate}[1.]
		%Task 1
		\begin{minipage}{0.5\textwidth}
			\item Two speakers are located in an x-y coordinate system at points $L_1(0|80)$ and $L_2(0|-80)$. The wave field created in the vicinity of the loudspeakers is examined using a pressure-sensitive microphone. The membranes of the two speakers oscillate simultaneously with the frequency $f=\SI{680}{\hertz}$. The microphone is moved from the point $A(1.50|0)$ along the line $g$. The intensity maxima and intensity minima of the pressure fluctuations are registered.
		\end{minipage}
		\hspace{0.5 cm}
		\begin{minipage}{0.5\textwidth}
			\includegraphics[width=6 cm]{lauti237}
		\end{minipage}
		
		\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item Explain in detail how an intensity maximum occurs.
			\item At the point $B(1.50|1.30)$ a second order intensity maximum is registered. Confirm that the speed of sound is $c=\SI{340}{\meter\over \second}$.
			\item Calculate the number of points on the line $g$ in which an intensity maximum can be detected.
		\end{enumerate}
		
		\item In a second attempt, only one loudspeaker is still in operation. This speaker is at the origin of the x-y coordinate system. The membrane of the loudspeaker oscillates at the frequency $f=\SI{680}{\hertz}$. The microphone is at a constant speed
		$\vec{v}_M$ moves to the right along the x-axis from point $A(1,50|0)$. The microphone registers pressure fluctuations with the constant frequency $f^*$.
		
		\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item Derive a formula with which the frequency $f^*$ can be calculated from $c,~f$ and the magnitude $v_M$ of the speed $\vec{v}_M$. Explain your considerations.
			\item Calculate $f^*$ for $v_M=\SI{2.00}{\meter\over \second}$.
			\item The microphone is now moved from the point at a constant speed $\vec{v}$
			$A(1.50|0)$ moves out on the line $g$ in the direction of increasing y-values. Explain that in this experiment the microphone registers pressure fluctuations, the frequency of which decreases as the distance between the microphone and the loudspeaker increases.
		\end{enumerate}
		
	\end{enumerate}
