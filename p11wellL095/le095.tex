	\noindent {\bf task 1.a)}\\
	\begin{minipage}{0.3\textwidth}
		In the sketch $d$ is the gap distance to be calculated $a={\SI{17.6}{\centi\meter}\over 2}=\SI{8.8}{\centi\meter}$ is the distance of the first maximum to the center of the screen and $l=\SI{2.80}{\meter}$ the distance of the screen to the double slit.
	\end{minipage}
	\begin{minipage}{0.7\textwidth}
		\includegraphics[width=10 cm]{beu95.pdf}
	\end{minipage}
	
	From the geometry of this arrangement it follows approximately for small angles:
	$$
	\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{2}
	\begin{array}{rcl}
		{\lambda \over d} &\approx& {a\over l} \quad (*)\\
		d &=& {l \over a} \cdot \lambda \\
		d &=& {\SI{2.80}{\meter} \over \SI{0.088}{\meter}}\cdot\SI{546e-9}{\meter}\\
		d &=& \SI{17e-6}{\meter} = 17\SI{}{\micro\meter}
	\end{array}
	$$
	
	The gap distance is therefore $\SI{17}{\micro\meter}$.
	\vspace{0.5 cm}
	
	\noindent {\bf task 1.b)}\\
	According to $(*)$ we have $a \sim \lambda$. This means that the distance between the intensity maxima on the screen also decreases with the wavelength. The number of observable maxima must therefore increase.
	\vspace{0.5 cm}
	
	\noindent{\bf Task 2.}\\
	We subtract the two equations and solve for l:
	$$
	\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{2}
	\begin{array}{rcll}
		{\lambda_1 \over d} &=& {a_1\over l}\\
		{\lambda_2 \over d} &=& {a_2\over l}\\
		\hline
		{\lambda_1 -\lambda_2 \over d} &=& {a_1-a_2 \over l}\\
		l &=& {(a_1-a_2)} \cdot {d \over \lambda_1 -\lambda_2}\\
		l &=& {\SI{0.003}{\meter}\cdot\SI{1e-4}{\meter} \over \SI{5.8959e-7}{\meter}-\SI{5.8899e- 7}{\meter}}\\
		l&=& \SI{500}{\meter}
	\end{array}
	$$
	The sodium double line cannot be separated with this arrangement.
